what is this

A revival of Electric Sheep: abstract creatures called sheep — each one a fractal flame, invented by Scott Draves — live, breed, and die by popular vote. This version has no server. The site is static files; everything else happens in the browsers of the people watching.

rendered, not served

No images are ever transmitted. A sheep is ~3 KB of genome JSON; your browser renders it with a Rust renderer compiled to WebAssembly. The renderer is deterministic: the same genome and seed produce byte-identical pixels on every machine. That one property is the foundation for everything below.

proof of sheep

Voting costs a render — specifically, one full loop of the sheep's animation. Your browser renders all 64 frames of the loop under a challenge derived from your key and the current generation, and signs the hash of each frame's work. The frames you just proved replay as an animation on the card: the work is the ballot, the art is the work, and the receipt is a dancing sheep. Anyone can later re-render any one frame (1/64th of the work) and catch a forged vote — your browser quietly does this to other peers' votes in the background, and a confirmed forgery becomes a fraud proof everyone can check, discrediting that key's votes everywhere. There is no vote server to trust. You can watch the machinery agree with itself on the determinism page. Votes also allocate beauty: every peer re-renders well-loved sheep at higher quality — since no pixels are ever shared, the flock's favorites sharpen on every screen at once, each machine doing its own deeper render of the same deterministic genome.

generations

Every 5 minutes a generation closes. The top sheep by net votes survive; they pair up and their children are born. Death is in your hands too: the ✕ button is a vote against — it costs the same render proof as a vote for (you must watch a sheep to condemn it), and any sheep voted net-negative is culled at the close. Each active generation also births two high-rate mutant clones of the top survivors and one immigrant — a fresh random genome derived from nothing but the generation number — so the gene pool never stagnates. Nobody computes any of this for you — every browser derives the same survivors, mutants, immigrants and children from the same votes, because breeding is deterministic too. That's also why the breeding preview is honest: pick any two living sheep and you see the exact child they would have this generation, before it's born. Releasing a sheep you bred costs a render proof, which doubles as your vote for it.

the swarm

Sheep and votes travel peer-to-peer as signed, immutable facts; every visitor stores what they've seen and forwards it to peers who missed it. The current build networks across browser tabs (open ?peer=2 beside the flock and watch them sync); the same protocol rides js-libp2p between strangers next.

Code: wasm-sheep — Rust → WASM renderer, vanilla JS, no frameworks, no build step, no server. Fractal flames by Scott Draves.
