what is this

A revival of Electric Sheep: abstract creatures called sheep — each one a fractal flame, invented by Scott Draves — live, breed, and die by popular vote. This version has no server. The site is static files; everything else happens in the browsers of the people watching.

rendered, not served

No images are ever transmitted. A sheep is ~3 KB of genome JSON; your browser renders it with a Rust renderer compiled to WebAssembly. The renderer is deterministic: the same genome and seed produce byte-identical pixels on every machine. That one property is the foundation for everything below.

proof of sheep

Voting costs a render. To vote for a sheep you render it with a challenge derived from your key and the current generation, and sign the hashes of the 64 chunks of work. Anyone can later re-render any one chunk (1/64th of the work) and catch a forged vote — and a confirmed forgery is itself provable to everyone else. There is no vote server to trust: the work is the ballot, and the art is the work. You can watch this machinery agree with itself on the determinism page.

generations

Every 5 minutes a generation closes. The top sheep by votes survive; they pair up and their children are born. Nobody computes this for you — every browser derives the same survivors and the same children from the same votes, because breeding is deterministic too. That's also why the breeding preview is honest: pick any two living sheep and you see the exact child they would have this generation, before it's born. Releasing a sheep you bred costs a render proof, which doubles as your vote for it.

the swarm

Sheep and votes travel peer-to-peer as signed, immutable facts; every visitor stores what they've seen and forwards it to peers who missed it. The current build networks across browser tabs (open ?peer=2 beside the flock and watch them sync); the same protocol rides js-libp2p between strangers next.

Code: wasm-sheep — Rust → WASM renderer, vanilla JS, no frameworks, no build step, no server. Fractal flames by Scott Draves.
